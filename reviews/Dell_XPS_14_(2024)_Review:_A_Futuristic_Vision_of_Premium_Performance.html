Dell XPS 14 (2024) Review: A Futuristic Vision of Premium Performance

🛒 Buy Now on Amazon

The Dell XPS 14 (2024) represents a bold design evolution in the world of premium ultraportables. Borrowing heavily from the futuristic aesthetics of the XPS 13 Plus, this 14.5-inch laptop features a seamless glass haptic touchpad and a capacitive touch function row. It bridges the gap between ultra-portability and high-performance computing, catering to creators and professionals alike. Equipped with Intel's latest Core Ultra processors, it delivers robust multi-threaded performance while improving on-chip AI processing tasks. The optional Nvidia GeForce RTX 4050 GPU provides a noticeable boost for light video editing and casual gaming. Users will immediately notice the breathtaking 3.2K OLED display, which offers vibrant colors and infinite contrast ratios. However, the controversial design choices, such as the flush keyboard and limited physical ports, may divide opinion. Despite these polarizing quirks, the laptop's build quality remains second to none with its CNC aluminum chassis. Ultimately, the XPS 14 is a premium, forward-looking machine that commands a high price tag for its cutting-edge design.

✅ Pros

  • Stunning premium CNC aluminum and Gorilla Glass design
  • Gorgeous optional 3.2K OLED display with butter-smooth 120Hz refresh rate
  • Strong CPU and optional RTX 4050 GPU performance for creative workflows
  • Exceptional audio quality from a powerful quad-speaker layout

❌ Cons

  • Polarizing capacitive touch function row and invisible haptic trackpad
  • Extremely limited port selection with no USB-A or HDMI ports built-in
  • Slightly heavier than most competitor 14-inch ultraportables

⚖️ Verdict

The Dell XPS 14 (2024) is a breathtakingly modern laptop that pushes the boundaries of traditional design, offering incredible OLED visuals and respectable creator-focused performance, though its avant-garde inputs require a definitive learning curve.

💡 Pro-Tip: If you plan to use this machine for creative work, make sure to opt for the Nvidia RTX 4050 configuration; the base integrated graphics model struggles with heavy 3D rendering and video encoding tasks.
