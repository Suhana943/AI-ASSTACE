Dell XPS 16 (2024) Review: The Ultimate Large-Screen Premium Powerhouse

🛒 Buy Now on Amazon

The Dell XPS 16 (2024) arrives as a bold reimagining of the classic premium Windows laptop, replacing the beloved XPS 15 with a larger, more futuristic design. Embracing the minimalist aesthetic pioneered by the XPS 13 Plus, this flagship machine features a seamless glass haptic touchpad and a capacitive touch function row. Under the hood, it pairs Intel's Core Ultra processors with powerful NVIDIA GeForce RTX 40-Series graphics to handle both demanding creative tasks and modern gaming. The gorgeous 16.3-inch OLED display option immediately steals the show with its infinite contrast, vibrant colors, and smooth 120Hz refresh rate. While its cutting-edge design is undeniably striking, the controversial keyboard layout and the complete reliance on USB-C ports may alienate some traditional users. Dell has optimized the thermal architecture to keep this thin chassis relatively cool, though the fans can still become audible under intense rendering workloads. Battery life is surprisingly resilient for a large-screen laptop of this caliber, easily powering through a standard workday of mixed productivity tasks. The integration of dedicated AI-acceleration hardware makes this laptop highly future-proof for next-generation software suites. Ultimately, the Dell XPS 16 represents a luxury computing experience that successfully justifies its high price tag for design enthusiasts and professionals alike.

✅ Pros

  • Stunning 16.3-inch 3.2K OLED display with vibrant colors and a smooth 120Hz refresh rate
  • Sleek, futuristic design with premium CNC aluminum construction and a seamless haptic glass touchpad
  • Excellent performance capability featuring Intel Core Ultra processors and dedicated NVIDIA RTX graphics
  • Superb audio experience with a powerful quad-speaker system delivering rich, clear sound

❌ Cons

  • The capacitive touch function row is less tactile and can be frustrating to use
  • Extremely limited port selection consisting only of USB-C/Thunderbolt 4 and a microSD slot
  • High starting price and expensive component upgrades place it strictly in the luxury tier

⚖️ Verdict

The Dell XPS 16 (2024) is a brilliant showcase of modern engineering that prioritizes futuristic design and high-end multimedia capabilities. It stands as a premium powerhouse tailored for creatives who want maximum screen real estate and robust performance without sacrificing style.

💡 Pro-Tip: If you do not require dedicated graphics for heavy gaming or 3D rendering, opt for the base model with Intel Arc graphics to save money and significantly extend your overall battery life.
