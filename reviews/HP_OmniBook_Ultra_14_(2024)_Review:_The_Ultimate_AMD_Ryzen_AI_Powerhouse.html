HP OmniBook Ultra 14 (2024) Review: The Ultimate AMD Ryzen AI Powerhouse

🛒 Buy Now on Amazon

The HP OmniBook Ultra 14 (2024) marks a bold new chapter for HP’s premium consumer laptop lineup, replacing the long-standing Spectre brand with a focus on cutting-edge AI capabilities. Powered by the groundbreaking AMD Ryzen AI 9 HX 375 processor, this ultraportable aims to deliver unmatched performance without sacrificing battery efficiency. Its sleek, minimalist chassis is crafted from high-quality aluminum, offering a rigid yet lightweight frame that is perfect for professionals on the move. Opening the lid reveals an exquisite 14-inch 2.2K touchscreen display that balances vibrant colors with excellent power conservation. HP has integrated an industry-leading 55 TOPS NPU into this machine, making it exceptionally future-proof for upcoming Windows Copilot+ features. During daily productivity workloads, the whisper-quiet cooling system ensures that thermal throttling never disrupts your creative flow. The keyboard remains a highlight of the experience, offering deep key travel and tactile feedback that rival the best business laptops on the market. While the port selection is slightly limited due to its ultra-slim profile, the inclusion of dual Thunderbolt 4 ports via AMD's latest chipset is a welcome surprise. Ultimately, the OmniBook Ultra 14 sets a new benchmark for what modern Windows ultraportables can achieve in the AI era.
